What Are Gallstones?

Gallstones are hardened deposits of digestive fluid that form in your gallbladder ‚Äî a small, pear-shaped organ beneath the liver that stores bile. When the balance of substances in bile becomes disrupted, they can crystallise and clump together into stones ranging from the size of a grain of sand to a golf ball.

Gallstones affect millions of people across India, particularly those above 40, women, and individuals with obesity or high-fat diets. When a stone blocks the bile duct, it causes severe upper-abdominal pain, nausea, and potentially dangerous infections. Conventional medicine typically recommends cholecystectomy (gallbladder removal surgery).
