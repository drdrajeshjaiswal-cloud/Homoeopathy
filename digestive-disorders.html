What Is Digestive Disorders?

The gut is often called the 'second brain' — and with good reason. The digestive system houses a vast network of neurons and immune cells, deeply connected to our emotional state, immune function, and overall well-being. When digestive health is compromised, the ripple effects are felt throughout the body.

Conventional gastroenterology offers antacids, laxatives, antispasmodics, and surgeries — providing symptomatic relief but rarely addressing the root cause. Many patients with IBS, GERD, or chronic constipation find themselves on medication indefinitely, with their condition gradually worsening.

Diet & Lifestyle for Digestive Health

✅ Recommended

  • High-fibre diet — fruits, vegetables, whole grains
  • Probiotics — curd, buttermilk, fermented foods
  • Adequate water — 8–10 glasses daily
  • Small, frequent meals eaten slowly
  • Warm water with lemon in the morning
  • Regular meal times and mindful eating

❌ Avoid or Reduce

  • Spicy, oily, and fried foods
  • Carbonated and cold drinks
  • Excessive tea and coffee
  • Eating late at night or lying down after meals
  • Eating under stress or while distracted
  • Refined foods and excess sugar
