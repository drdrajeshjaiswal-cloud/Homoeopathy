What Is Women's Health?

Women's health encompasses a rich and complex interplay of hormonal, emotional, physical, and social factors. From the challenges of adolescent menstrual irregularity to the hormonal shifts of menopause, women's bodies undergo profound changes that deserve gentle, individualised care.

Conventional gynaecology often defaults to hormonal contraceptives, metformin, or surgical procedures for many women's health conditions. While these have their place, many women seek a more natural approach — one that honours the body's wisdom rather than overriding it with synthetic hormones.

Constitutional homoeopathy is uniquely suited to women's health — its individualised approach accounts for the deeply personal nature of hormonal, emotional, and reproductive health. Nutrition & Lifestyle for Women's Health

✅ Recommended

  • Iron-rich foods during menstruation — dates, spinach, jaggery
  • Phytoestrogen foods for menopause — flaxseed, soy
  • Regular exercise — yoga particularly beneficial for PCOS
  • Stress management and adequate sleep
  • Vitamin D and calcium for bone health
  • Low-glycaemic diet for hormonal balance

❌ Avoid or Reduce

  • Excess sugar and processed foods (worsens PCOS)
  • Caffeine (worsens PMS and hot flushes)
  • Cold drinks during periods (increases cramping)
  • Excess dairy (linked to endometriosis flares in some women)
  • Smoking and alcohol
  • Skipping meals or extreme calorie restriction

Frequently Asked Questions

Can homoeopathy help me get pregnant with PCOS?

Yes. Many women with PCOS have achieved natural conception after constitutional homoeopathic treatment that regulated their cycles, improved ovulation, and balanced hormones. Results depend on individual factors including age and duration of infertility.

Is homoeopathy safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Constitutional homoeopathic treatment is safe throughout pregnancy and can help with morning sickness, anxiety, back pain, and gestational hypertension.

Can homoeopathy treat uterine fibroids?

Homoeopathy is effective for managing fibroid-related symptoms (heavy bleeding, pain) and may reduce fibroid size in some cases. Large fibroids causing pressure symptoms may still require surgical evaluation.

How long does PCOS treatment take?

Initial menstrual cycle regulation is often seen within 2–3 months. Comprehensive treatment for PCOS typically takes 9–18 months for lasting hormonal balance.
