What Is Psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune skin condition that causes skin cells to multiply up to ten times faster than normal. As cells pile up on the surface, they form the characteristic red, scaly plaques ‚Äî patches of raised, inflamed skin covered with silvery-white scales that can crack, bleed, and itch intensely.

Psoriasis affects over 125 million people worldwide and is particularly challenging because it is not merely a skin problem ‚Äî it is a systemic immune disorder with its roots deep in the body's internal imbalance. Steroid creams and immunosuppressant drugs may suppress symptoms temporarily, but they do not address the underlying cause, and symptoms almost always return when medication is stopped ‚Äî often worse than before.

Types of Psoriasis We Treat

Most Common ‚Äî 80%

Plaque Psoriasis

Raised, inflamed patches covered with silvery-white scales. Most often appears on elbows, knees, scalp, and lower back. Can be mild or cover large areas of the body.

Scalp Type

Scalp Psoriasis

Thick, adherent scales on the scalp that may extend to the forehead, back of the neck, or ears. Often confused with severe dandruff and causes significant hair fall.

Droplet Shape

Guttate Psoriasis

Small, drop-shaped lesions scattered over the trunk, arms, and legs. Often triggered by a streptococcal infection and more common in children and young adults.

Nail Involvement

Nail Psoriasis

Causes pitting, discolouration, thickening, and separation of nails. Affects up to 50% of psoriasis patients and is often a precursor to psoriatic arthritis.

Joint Involvement

Psoriatic Arthritis

An inflammatory arthritis associated with psoriasis, causing joint pain, stiffness, and swelling. Homoeopathy effectively addresses both the skin and joint components together.

Skin Folds

Inverse / Pustular

Inverse psoriasis appears in skin folds (armpits, groin, under breasts). Pustular psoriasis presents as pus-filled blisters ‚Äî both require careful constitutional treatment.
