What Is Migraines & Headaches?

Migraines are not merely headaches — they are complex neurological events that can be utterly debilitating, stealing hours or days from a person's life with each episode. For many sufferers, the fear of the next migraine becomes a source of anxiety itself, profoundly affecting quality of life.

Conventional neurology offers triptans, beta-blockers, antidepressants, and anti-epileptics for migraine prevention — each with their own side effect profiles and variable efficacy. Many patients develop medication overuse headaches from frequent analgesic use, creating a cruel cycle of pain.

Constitutional homoeopathy offers a genuinely different approach — one that works with the individual's unique migraine pattern, triggers, and constitution to reduce episodes from within. Migraine Triggers & Lifestyle Tips

✅ Recommended

  • Regular meal times — skipping meals triggers migraines
  • Adequate hydration throughout the day
  • Consistent sleep and wake times (even weekends)
  • Magnesium-rich foods — pumpkin seeds, spinach, almonds
  • Regular gentle exercise (yoga, walking)
  • Migraine diary — tracking triggers and patterns

❌ Avoid or Reduce

  • Common food triggers: aged cheese, red wine, dark chocolate
  • Monosodium glutamate (MSG) in Chinese food
  • Caffeine in excess or sudden withdrawal
  • Irregular sleep patterns and oversleeping
  • Bright screens and fluorescent lighting during headaches
  • Skipping meals or prolonged fasting
