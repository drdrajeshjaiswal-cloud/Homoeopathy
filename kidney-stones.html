What Are Kidney Stones?

Kidney stones are hard mineral and salt deposits that form inside the kidneys when urine becomes highly concentrated. As urine becomes saturated with minerals like calcium, oxalate, and uric acid, these substances can crystallise and bind together ‚Äî forming stones that range from a tiny grain of sand to the size of a golf ball.

When a stone begins to move through the narrow urinary tract, it can trigger excruciating pain, blockages, and complications. Most conventional treatments involve waiting for the stone to pass, medications, or invasive surgical procedures. Uric Acid Stones
